Sleep Study

A Level 3 sleep study can help evaluate sleep-related breathing concerns such as snoring, pauses in breathing, and suspected obstructive sleep apnea.

When a sleep study helps

A sleep study may be considered for loud snoring, witnessed pauses in breathing, choking during sleep, morning headaches, daytime sleepiness, poor concentration, resistant blood pressure, or persistent fatigue.

What Level 3 polysomnography means

Level 3 testing generally records key breathing and oxygen-related signals during sleep. The result helps the doctor understand whether sleep apnea or another sleep breathing issue is likely.

After the report

The doctor explains the report, severity, health relevance, and treatment options such as lifestyle measures, CPAP/BIPAP discussion, or further evaluation when needed.
